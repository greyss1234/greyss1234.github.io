The Best Danish Blackbread You've Ever Tasted

Author: Greyson Wrobel

All you need to know to make the most authentic, traditional Danish blackbread (rye bread) there is! Follow these simple steps and recipe which has been passed down through generations of a Danish family to warm your home for the winter.

All materials linked at the bottom of the page. Danish translation provided above.

Picture of blackbread -- not made using recipe.

Ingredients

  • Ryebread starter (1 jar)
  • Lukewarm water (1750mL)
  • Salt (3TBSP)
  • Rye flour (1000g)
  • All-purpose flour (750g)
  • Lukewarm water (500mL)
  • Rye kernels (1000g)

    • Instructions

    1. (Preliminarily) Make sure to let the rye starter sit out for about half a day and become room temperature.
    2. In a big bowl, Mix the full jar of rye starter with lukewarm water. Stir until fully dissolved and add salt.
    3. Add the two types of flour simultaneously whilst stirring, best done with two people.
    4. Once all combined, cover with tin foil and let sit for 12-24 hours.
    5. The next day, add the lukewarm water and mix, followed by the rye kernels. Combine thoroughly.
    6. In order to preserve the rye starter, fill up a jar with the dough and sprinkle with salt. This can be done either before or after adding the rye kernels. Set in refridgerator.
    7. Separate the dough into two baking forms and cover with tinfoil. Let sit for another day.
    8. Finally, bake the bread for two hours at 392 degrees F. Opptionally, set the oven to broil for no more than a minute to give the bread an extra crunch on top. Enjoy!
